Prince

Liberian novelist & screenwriter

Born in Monrovia, raised between the rubble of civil war and the resilience of family stories, Prince writes for the page and the screen with one goal: to show Liberia as it is, and as it dreams to be.

My Story

I grew up listening to my grandmother tell stories under a mango tree in Paynesville. Those stories — of spirits, survival, and sly palm wine tappers — became the foundation of everything I write. When I discovered cinema at a makeshift community screening of old Nollywood films, I realized that stories could move and speak and be seen by the world.

After studying literature at the University of Liberia, I worked as a radio scriptwriter and later as a freelance journalist. My first novel, The Palm Wine Thief, was born from a whispered family secret. My first short film script, Clay Hands, was produced in 2023 and screened at the Monrovia International Film Festival.

Today, I split my time between writing novels that explore Liberian identity and crafting screenplays that demand to be seen. I believe our stories belong on global shelves and global screens — but first, they must be true to us.

Selected recognition: Finalist, Afritondo Short Story Prize (2022); Development Grant, African Screenwriters Lab (2024).

My Liberia

Liberia is not just my setting — it's my language. The rhythm of market women's banter, the smell of burnt plantains after rain, the way we say "how far?" when we mean "how are you?" — these details belong in every scene I write. Whether it's a novel or a script, my work carries Monrovia's dust and the sea breeze of Robertsport.

I am committed to mentoring young Liberian writers through free workshops (inquire via contact page).
